HEALTH, WELLNESS AND INSURANCE BENEFITS POLICY
Version: 2.1
Effective Date: January 2026
Department: HR – Benefits
Classification: Company‑Wide Policy

1. PURPOSE
This policy summarizes the company’s health, wellness, and insurance benefits, with focus on access to care, financial protection, and preventive wellbeing programs.

2. MEDICAL, DENTAL AND VISION COVERAGE
- Eligible employees may enroll in company‑sponsored health plans as defined in the benefits guide.
- Plans typically include medical, prescription drug, and mental health coverage.
- Dental and vision benefits may be offered separately.
- Coverage levels, employee contributions, and eligibility rules are communicated annually during enrollment and stored in the benefits portal.

3. MENTAL HEALTH COVERAGE
- Health plans must cover mental health and substance use services in line with applicable parity laws and internal standards.
- Covered services may include outpatient therapy, psychiatric care, and inpatient or intensive programs where clinically required.
- Employees should consult the benefits documentation or provider for details on co‑pays, authorizations, and networks.

4. WELLNESS PROGRAMS
The company may offer:
- Health risk assessments and screenings where allowed by law
- Fitness or gym subsidies or onsite facilities
- Wellness campaigns focused on sleep, nutrition, movement, and stress
- Educational sessions on managing chronic conditions and healthy habits

Participation is voluntary. Any data collected through wellness programs is handled in compliance with privacy laws and internal data protection policies.

5. LIFE AND DISABILITY INSURANCE
- Basic life insurance and long‑term disability (LTD) coverage may be provided at no cost to employees, subject to eligibility rules.
- Employees may elect additional life or disability coverage for themselves and eligible dependents, at their own cost.
- These benefits are intended to provide financial protection in case of serious illness, disability, or death.

Details, including benefit amounts and eligibility, are set out in official plan documents.

6. EMPLOYEE ASSISTANCE PROGRAM (EAP)
As described in the Mental Health and Wellness Policy, the EAP is a core wellbeing benefit funded by the company. It offers confidential counseling, crisis support, and practical guidance for personal and work‑related issues, at no charge to employees.

7. COMMUNICATION AND CHANGES
Benefits and eligibility may change based on legal, regulatory, or business requirements. The company will:
- Communicate material changes in a timely manner
- Provide updated benefit summaries at least annually
- Offer information sessions or help channels during enrollment

If there is any conflict between this policy and formal plan documents or governing laws, the plan documents and laws take precedence.
