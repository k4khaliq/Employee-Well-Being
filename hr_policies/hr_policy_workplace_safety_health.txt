WORKPLACE SAFETY, HEALTH AND ERGONOMICS POLICY
Version: 3.0
Effective Date: January 2026
Department: Workplace Experience & HR
Classification: Company‑Wide Policy

1. PURPOSE
This policy defines the company’s commitment to a safe and healthy workplace. It covers safety responsibilities, hazard control, ergonomics, incident reporting, and emergency preparedness for all employees and locations, including remote work where reasonably practicable.

2. SAFETY PRINCIPLES
- Safety is a core business value and leadership responsibility.
- Incidents and near misses are reported, investigated, and learned from.
- Prevention is prioritized over reaction.
- Employees have the right to refuse unsafe work without retaliation.

3. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
3.1 Leadership
- Set safety expectations and allocate resources.
- Review safety performance regularly.
- Support corrective actions and improvements.

3.2 Safety Committee
- Represent multiple functions and sites.
- Review incident and hazard data.
- Recommend improvements and track agreed actions.

3.3 Managers
- Ensure compliance with safety procedures and training.
- Address reported hazards promptly.
- Reinforce safe behaviors through feedback and recognition.

3.4 Employees
- Follow safety rules and procedures.
- Use required protective equipment correctly.
- Report hazards, incidents, and near misses immediately.
- Participate in safety training and drills.

4. HAZARD IDENTIFICATION AND CONTROL
4.1 Inspections and Assessments
- Regular inspections are carried out using standard checklists.
- Job‑specific risk assessments are performed for higher‑risk tasks.
- Findings are documented with owners, due dates, and follow‑up.

4.2 Hierarchy of Controls
Hazards are controlled in this order of preference:
1) Eliminate the hazard
2) Substitute safer materials or methods
3) Use engineering controls
4) Use administrative controls (procedures, training, scheduling)
5) Use personal protective equipment (PPE)

5. ERGONOMICS AND WORKSTATIONS
5.1 Office and Remote Workstations
Employees should have:
- Stable seating with appropriate back support
- A desk or surface at suitable height
- Screens positioned at comfortable height and distance
- Keyboard and mouse aligned to reduce strain
- Adequate lighting and minimal glare

Ergonomic assessments may be requested for office and remote setups when needed.

5.2 Breaks and Movement
Employees are encouraged to:
- Take short movement breaks at least every 60–90 minutes
- Vary posture and avoid extended static positions
- Use tools and reminders to support healthy habits

Managers should not discourage reasonable breaks in the name of productivity.

6. PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE)
- Where risk assessments require PPE, the company will provide it at no cost.
- Employees must use, maintain, and store PPE as trained.
- Damaged or missing PPE must be reported and replaced before work continues.
- Repeated failure to use required PPE may lead to corrective action.

7. INCIDENT, NEAR‑MISS AND HAZARD REPORTING
7.1 What to Report
- Work‑related injuries and illnesses
- Near misses that could have caused harm
- Property damage from unsafe conditions
- Observed hazards or unsafe behavior

7.2 How to Report
- Inform your manager or safety contact immediately.
- Record the incident or hazard in the designated reporting tool as soon as possible.
- For emergencies, contact local emergency services first, then internal contacts.

7.3 Investigation
- All incidents and serious near misses are investigated.
- Focus is on root causes and system improvements, not blame.
- Corrective actions are documented and monitored to completion.

8. EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS
The company maintains emergency plans appropriate to each site, including:
- Fire and evacuation procedures
- Medical emergency response
- Severe weather or natural disaster procedures where relevant
- Security threats and workplace violence response

Employees must:
- Know primary and secondary evacuation routes and assembly points
- Follow instructions from emergency wardens and responders
- Participate in required drills

9. HEALTH PROMOTION
The company encourages:
- Use of available wellness programs and health screenings
- Open dialogue about safety, health, and wellbeing
- Integration of physical and mental health considerations into work design and project planning

10. REVIEW AND CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT
Safety data, audit results, and employee feedback are reviewed at least annually. This policy is updated when regulations, risks, or operations change significantly.
